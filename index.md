My doctoral thesis, *Murmurs of Affect, Queer Sexuality, and (Extra-)Archival Practice in the German Democratic Republic, 1979–1990* (University of Birmingham, 2025, passed without corrections), examines how queer individuals navigated, subverted, and created archives under the surveillance apparatus of the GDR. A revised monograph is forthcoming with Peter Lang Verlag as part of the **Emerging Scholars Competition in Queer Studies – German Studies** (Gold Open Access / OAPEN).

My research has been supported by the Wolfson Postgraduate Scholarship, the Association for German Studies, and Women+ in German Studies. I have presented at international conferences including SECAC (Cincinnati), the University of Bergen, the University of Cambridge, and the University of Liverpool.

Previously, I held a doctoral placement at the **Victoria & Albert Museum**, London with the Bert Hardy Collection, and prior to this, spent three years at Mellors & Kirk Fine Art Auctioneers.

I received my M.A. (Distinction) from the **Courtauld Institute of Art** and my B.A. (First Class) from the **University of Nottingham**.
