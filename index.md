I am an art historian specialising in East German visual culture, queer history, memory studies, and archival practice. My work sits at the intersection of art history, gender studies, and Cold War history.

My doctoral thesis, *Murmurs of Affect, Queer Sexuality, and (Extra-)Archival Practice in the German Democratic Republic, 1979–1990* (University of Birmingham, 2025, passed without corrections), examines how queer individuals navigated, subverted, and created archives under the surveillance apparatus of the GDR. A revised version is forthcoming with Peter Lang Verlag as part of the **Emerging Scholars Competition in Queer Studies – German Studies** (Gold Open Access / OAPEN).

My research has been supported by the Wolfson Postgraduate Scholarship, the Association for German Studies, and Women+ in German Studies. I have presented at international conferences including SECAC (Cincinnati), the University of Cambridge, and the University of Liverpool.

Previously, I held a doctoral placement at the **Victoria & Albert Museum, London**, with the Bert Hardy Collection, and have gained curatorial experience at the Stephen Friedman Gallery, London.

I received my M.A. (Distinction) from the **Courtauld Institute of Art** and my B.A. (First Class) from the **University of Nottingham**.
